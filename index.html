  • Understanding Probate in New York

    • Probate is the legal process of administering a deceased person’s estate. It involves proving the validity of the will in court, paying debts, and distributing the remaining assets to heirs.

    The Probate Process:

  • Filing a petition in Surrogate's Court

  • Notifying heirs and beneficiaries

  • Inventorying the estate's assets

  • Paying off debts and taxes

  • Distributing the remaining property

    • This process can be time-consuming and complicated, especially when there is no will or when disputes arise. A skilled probate attorney can simplify and expedite the process.

    • Key Estate Planning Documents

    To fully protect yourself and your family, your estate plan should include the following documents:

    1. Last Will and Testament

    This document outlines how your property should be distributed and who will care for minor children. A will must go through probate.

    2. Living Trust

    A revocable living trust can help you avoid probate and maintain privacy. It also allows for faster distribution of assets.

    3. Durable Power of Attorney

    Assigns someone you trust to manage your finances if you become incapacitated.

    4. Healthcare Proxy and Living Will

    Designates someone to make medical decisions on your behalf and outlines your preferences for end-of-life care.
